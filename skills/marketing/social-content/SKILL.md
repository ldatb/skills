Build the durable content engine a founder posts from every week, not a one-off batch of posts. The engine is the asset: pillars decide what to say, formats decide how to shape it per platform, cadence decides when, and the give-before-you-sell ratio decides whether the audience stays. Live trend research belongs to the separate `social-media-viral` skill — this skill stays the durable system, and trend-scraping is out of scope here.

Read [the content system reference](references/content-system.md) before drafting: pillars, the hook-retain-reward structure, format fit, cadence, repurposing, the give/sell ratio, failure modes, red flags, and a worked example.

## Steps

1. **Pick the pillars.** Name 3 to 5 content pillars at the intersection of founder expertise, audience pain, and what the product earns the right to sell. Reject a candidate pillar that only the founder cares about, and reject one with no link to a buying decision. This step is done when 3 to 5 named pillars exist and each one carries a one-line audience payoff.

2. **Choose formats per platform.** Map each pillar to its best-fit format on each platform, drawn from the format-fit table in the reference: X favors the standalone post and the thread, Instagram favors the carousel, the Reel, and the story. This step is done when the 3 to 5 pillars each carry at least one named format on Instagram and one on X.

3. **Draft with hooks.** Write each post on the hook-retain-reward spine: a first line that stops the scroll, a middle that holds attention, a close that pays the reader off. Reject any draft whose opening line states a topic instead of a tension. This step is done when each draft has a distinct hook line, a retain body, and an explicit reward.

4. **Schedule a cadence.** Place the drafts on a weekly calendar at a frequency the founder can sustain unaided, with pillars rotated so no single theme dominates a week. The reference gives a starter cadence per platform. This step is done when a 7-day calendar names a pillar, a format, and a platform in every planned slot.

5. **Repurpose one idea across formats.** Take one strong insight and recut it into the platform-native formats from the repurposing section — one X thread, one IG carousel, one Reel script, one story prompt — rather than cross-posting identical text. This step is done when one source insight has spawned at least three distinct platform-native pieces.

6. **Review against the give/sell ratio.** Count the value posts against the promotional posts across the week and confirm the give-before-you-sell ratio holds at roughly four-to-one or more generous. Check the drafted week against the failure-modes and red-flags lists in the reference. This step is done when the week clears the ratio threshold and trips zero red flags.
