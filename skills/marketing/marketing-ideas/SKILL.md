Generate marketing and growth ideas against a stated goal, then rank them honestly so the few that ship are the few most likely to move the metric. Ideas are cheap; the founder's time to build and run them is the scarce resource, so prioritization is the point, not an afterthought. A growth thesis sits above the work and an off-thesis idea is a distraction wearing the costume of progress.

Generation and selection are two different modes — diverge to volume first, then converge to a ranked few. The generation frameworks, the divergent-then-convergent discipline, the ICE and RICE models with honest-scoring rules, the failure modes, the red flags, and a worked example live in [references/idea-generation.md](references/idea-generation.md) — read that reference before step 2.

## Steps

1. **Frame the goal and the constraint.** State the growth thesis in one sentence (the mechanism this business bets on), the one metric that mechanism moves, the audience, and the real budget or time constraint. This step is done when thesis, metric, audience, and constraint are each written in one line.

2. **Diverge — generate many ideas.** Run several generation frameworks as separate prompts (the channel list / Bullseye, growth loops vs funnels, jobs-to-be-done angles, competitor teardown, 10x not 10%), deferring all scoring, until a raw count of at least 15 ideas exists. This step is done when 15 or more raw ideas are listed and at least two would 10x the metric.

3. **Cluster the raw list.** Group the ideas by channel or growth mechanism, name each cluster, cut duplicates, and drop ideas that serve no thesis. This step is done when the raw list collapses to 6–8 distinct bets, each tagged with its growth mode (loop or funnel).

4. **Score with ICE.** Score each surviving bet on Impact, Confidence, and Ease (1–10), anchoring confidence to cited evidence and estimating whole-lifecycle effort, then rank by the product. This step is done when every bet carries three scores, an ICE value, and a one-line reason, with the same model applied across all.

5. **Pick the top few.** Commit to the highest-ranked bets the constraint allows and mark the tail explicitly as not-now. This step is done when the picks fit the stated budget or time constraint and the deferred ideas are named, not silently dropped.

6. **Define a success metric per pick.** Attach one quantified metric and a numeric threshold with a time window to each picked idea, so each test returns a verdict. This step is done when each pick names one metric and one threshold.

7. **Screen against the failure modes.** Check the result against chasing tactics without a thesis, shiny-channel syndrome, no prioritization, and ideas with no success metric, plus the red-flag list in the reference. This step is done when the output clears all four failure modes and no red flag remains open.
