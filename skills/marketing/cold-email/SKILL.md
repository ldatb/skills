Write cold outbound that a stranger answers: the right person, an opener proving the email was written for them, one pain they already feel, one outcome, and one easy next step. Craft decides the reply rate far more than the offer does — so the work is precision, not volume.

This skill **drafts** emails and sequences only. Sending is an external mutation against real recipients and the sender's domain reputation, so the user reviews and sends every message; this skill never sends and never schedules a send. The depth behind each step lives in [the cold-email playbook](references/cold-email-playbook.md).

## Steps

1. **Define the ICP and the trigger.** State the one buyer persona (role, company size, industry) and the one observable event that makes outreach timely (a hire, a launch, a funding round, a public quote). Done when a named persona and a named trigger are both written down, with no second persona smuggled in.

2. **Choose the tier and research the opener.** Pick the personalization tier — none, segment, or 1:1 — from the deal size and list size per the playbook's tier table, then gather the specific fact the opener will cite. Done when the chosen tier is recorded and the opener fact is specific enough to be false for most other companies on the list.

3. **Draft the one-CTA email.** Write the six parts in order — subject, opener, problem, value, single CTA, compliant sign-off — in 50–125 words, reader-centered. Done when the draft contains exactly one ask and the "you/your" count is at least the "we/our" count.

4. **Run the spam and deliverability check.** Audit the draft against the playbook's six-trigger list: at most one link, no attachment, no banned spam words, at most one exclamation point, plain-text body, intact merge tags. Done when those six checks pass, recorded as a pass.

5. **Build the follow-up sequence.** Add three to four touches on the playbook cadence (days 0, +3, +7, +14), each shorter than the last, each carrying one new angle, each with its own single CTA, ending in a break-up touch. Done when the sequence has a break-up touch and no touch reads as a bare "bumping this".

6. **Run the compliance check.** Verify the CAN-SPAM and GDPR floor from the playbook: real sender identity, a non-deceptive subject, a working opt-out in every message, and a physical postal address. Done when each of those four items is present in every touch, recorded as a pass.

## Notes

The skill stops at a reviewed draft sequence. Handing the messages to a sending tool, an email API, or an automation platform is the user's decision and the user's action.
