Build the asset a seller carries into a specific deal moment, not a generic brochure. A buyer signs when the value is obvious, the proof is credible, and the objection in their head is already answered. Feature lists and adjective-heavy decks do none of that, so lead with the buyer's outcome and back every claim with evidence.

Work the deal from blocker to packaged asset. The reference carries the asset taxonomy, the objection-handling framework, battlecard and proof structures, ROI framing, the failure modes, and a worked example: [enablement-assets.md](references/enablement-assets.md).

## Steps

1. **Locate the deal stage and the blocker.** Name the funnel stage (awareness, evaluation, justification, or competitive bake-off) and the one thing stopping the next step. The asset is chosen once the stage and the single blocker are both written down.

2. **Pick the asset that clears that blocker.** Match the blocker to its asset using the taxonomy in [enablement-assets.md](references/enablement-assets.md) — one-pager for a cold intro, narrative for a pitch, battlecard for a competitor, ROI model for budget sign-off, case study for trust. The step is done when the chosen asset names the blocker it removes.

3. **Draft it value-led.** Open on the buyer's outcome — the problem solved, the cost avoided, the gain captured — and demote features to evidence beneath each claim. The draft passes when its headline states a buyer outcome and no claim leads with a feature.

4. **Attach proof to every claim.** Bind each assertion to a named customer result, a quantified metric, a quote, or a third-party signal, following the proof structures in [enablement-assets.md](references/enablement-assets.md). The step is complete when no value claim stands without evidence beside it.

5. **Answer the deal's live objections.** Run the top blockers (price, timing, trust, status quo, competitor) through acknowledge → reframe → evidence → confirm, drawing the patterns from [enablement-assets.md](references/enablement-assets.md). The step is done when each likely objection has a written response ending in a confirming question.

6. **Package for the seller.** Strip jargon, cap the one-pager at a single page, lead the deck with the narrative arc, and add a one-line "use this when" note to the asset. Packaging is complete when a rep can open the asset and deliver it without rewriting a word.
